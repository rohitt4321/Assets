Industrial Syllabus

It is generally felt that there is a wide gap between education and industry requirements. Students often complain that they are not properly prepared for the workplace after completing their studies.

In order to bridge this gap, it is important for educators to understand the needs of industry and align their syllabi accordingly. Only then can students be adequately prepared for the challenges they will face in the real world.

Some key areas that should be addressed in the syllabus are: - Communication skills - Teamwork - Problem solving - Critical thinking - Time management - Conflict resolution - Interpersonal skills

Placement Assistance

The placement assistance service is designed to help students find suitable placements for their desired profession, and to help employers find the right candidates. It will be a win-win situation for both the students and the employers.

In order to get a better understanding of what this service is about, it is important to know what placement assistance does. The placement assistance service enables students to find placements in a specific domain, according to their interests and skillsets. Students can also use it as a platform for getting advice on how they should prepare themselves before finding a job or internship position. Employers can use this service as well, but with different intentions: they can use it as an opportunity to get in touch with potential new employees or interns that they might not have found otherwise.

The future placement support will be mainly focused on job satisfaction. This will ensure that students are happy with their work, so they will be more likely to stay in the company after their placement is over.
